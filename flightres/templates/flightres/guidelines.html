UAV Guidelines

Anyone applying for drone permissions can get their drone permit from chief district office of the proposed project location, if they are flying a drone less than 2 km.

Government of Nepal has different concern for drone operation.

Civil Aviation Authority of Nepal (CAAN )

CAAN is mainly concerned about collision with other aircrafts i.e. safety. They believe that drone flying poses security threats and weakens air safety.

Ministry of Information and Communication

They are concerned about the frequency of the remote control of the device used. This frequency might create interference with devices being used by security personnel and communication.

Home Ministry

Drones initially were used for military purposes to strike attacks remotely in different areas. While drones being used today carry payloads in the form of different optical and other kinds of sensors. As they are capable of carrying other payloads when tweaked, the home ministry concerns the illegal and unethical use of drones encroaching state’s safety and personal privacy.

Relevant Line Ministries

Drones in its original terms means the vehicles which are being used by military to strike weapons in different areas. As such, the home ministry which looks after country’s security is worried if people use drones for illegal activities.

"Beside above agencies, line agencies needs also to be contacted for drone permissions eg. National parks department for flying in protected areas, ministry of tourism for movie shoot in mountains.

As per the recent regulations of Nepal, the permissions are granted to the local government. So any agencies can give the permissions according to the flight plan and spot."

Things to note while operating UAVs in Nepal

  • check_circle Free to fly UAV under 200m (656 feet) of under 2 kg (4.4 lb) on your personal properties like inside your house, land, and garden.
  • check_circle Unmanned aircraft shall be permitted to be operated in day VMC conditions only.
  • check_circle If the operations are to be conducted in a Restricted or Prohibited area, prior approval from the concerned Authority is required before applying for flight permission
  • check_circle In Nepal, permissions are for a short period of time ( a few days to 3 months) and not for a longer period of time.
  • check_circle You must never fly near other aircraft.
  • check_circle Do not fly your drone over heritage sites, national parks, conservation areas, military installations, power plants or other NO FLY ZONES that could cause concern among the local authorities
  • check_circle Do not fly within 50 meters (164 feet) of any vessel, vehicle, or structure.
  • check_circle Airport areas including take-off and approach paths shall be free of all UAV activity at all times regardless of altitude.
  • check_circle Drone must be flown within the visual line of sight (normally within 500 m horizontally and 400 feet vertically) of its remote pilot (the person in charge of operating the drone). Operations beyond these distances are not allowed.
